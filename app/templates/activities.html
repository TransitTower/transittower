In Transit Tower guesthouse we go beyond ordinary snorkeling or fishing experiences. Our diverse range of activities ensures there’s something for everyone seeking unforgettable moments on this paradise island.

Excursion / Island Hopping

Immerse yourself in the local culture and enjoy the wonders of island hopping in the Maldives. Explore the rich culture, heritage and history of the connecting chain of islands In Addu Atoll. Our neighbours here offer a variety of traditional, local crafts and souvenirs, as well as delicious local cuisine. In respect of how our neighbouring local Maldivians work and live together on the island, the required dress code for the excursion is, at minimum, a short-sleeved t-shirt, knee-length shorts for women, and shorts for men. A parent must accompany all children under the age of 16.

Snorkeling

Our world-class reefs hosts a breathtaking variety of marine life. The reef is easily accessible, just a few metres from the shore, and perfect for beginners to explore. It gently slopes to a depth of 20m and is home to spectacular coral, harmless blacktip sharks, turtles, rays and many other species. Wade into the lagoon with your mask, snorkel and fins and discover a world of mesmerising underwater beauty.

Day Fishing

Permit, Barracuda, Giant Trevally, Tuna, Groupers, Snappers, Billfish – you’ll find them all! Tropical fish in the Maldives are numerous. You can reel in specimens of all shapes and colors, including plenty you’ve never seen before! There are endless catches to make, but here are a few of the top targets that bring anglers to the Maldives. The Maldives are among a few places in the world where you can catch GTs year-round, both on the reefs and over the flats. So, targeting GTs here is a great opportunity to try out the Maldives’ most popular fishing methods – jigging and popping. The same goes when fishing over the flats, the only caveat being that the fish here are smaller, usually hitting between 10–15 pounds. Fly fishers won’t mind, though, as the clear shallow waters here are perfect for sight casting.

Night Fishing

Experience fishing in the Maldivian style with hand lines and small fish as bait; an art practised by many generations in the country. Includes a large spool, fishing line, and bait. The line is first dropped to the water in a place where there are lots of fish, particularly near corals. Although there is no off-season for this nighttime expedition, it would be advisable to go night fishing when there are clear skies.

Diving

The Maldives is a low-lying nation with dreamy white sand beaches and excellent reefs with an abundance of marine life. Scuba diving is done at a leisurely drift pace as the Indian monsoon current sweeps along the island chains, moving nutrients and divers along. This rich water flows up along the walls, feeding the soft coral clinging to the rock sides. Inside the atoll lagoons, rock pinnacles – thila – rise up from the depths to scratch the water’s surface. In the channels there are swim-through, caverns and overhangs festooned with colorful sponges, invertebrates and gorgons. At cleaning stations, wrasse and shrimp service manta rays, with giant turtle and whale shark sightings common.
